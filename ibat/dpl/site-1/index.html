Basic website

A basic website is made up of one to five pages, and requires only very simple functionality. Think of it as a modern day business card.

In addition to the core pages, basic websites will have a contact and landing page.

Even a basic website should also be mobile responsive, but it’s always worth checking that this is included in the design price.

Basic websites are great if you’re after a very simple, stripped back online presence.

A basic website is a great starting point to showcase your business online, and receive enquiries.

Upfront costs

  • Design £200 to £500
  • Domain £2.99 to £100 / year
  • Total:  £202.99 to £600

Ongoing costs

  • Hosting £3 to £45 / month
  • Content Updates £0 to £10 / month
  • Total:  £3 to £55 / month

Small business website

For businesses that are likely to update their content regularly. A great choice for startups, freelancers or any growing organisation.

Small business websites are also brochure style, but are far more feature-rich.

They will also have more pages than a basic website, and will be designed to be mobile responsive.

The extra features on small business websites typically include:

  • Integrated social media
  • A content management system
  • Google Analytics, Google Maps and Google My Business

Upfront costs

  • Design £500 to £1,000
  • Domain £2.99 to £100 / year
  • Total:  £502.99 to £1,100

Ongoing costs

  • Hosting £3 to £45 / month
  • Content Updates £0 to £30 / month
  • Total:  £3 to £75 / month

eCommerce website

eCommerce websites can vary massively in price. So you really need to think about what functionality you want from your new site.

Do you want your ecommerce website make up most of your brand’s presence – do you want people to hang around, to read blog posts, to engage with your social media? Do you expect people to return to your site?

Or will your website be more of a stripped back ‘one-stop shop‘? Will your customers shop mosly on mobile or on desktop?

The price of an ecommerce website is impacted by the number of items you want to stock, and the number of varieties of each item that are available.

You can expect an ecommerce web design to include an order management system, delivery tracking functionality and a live chat facility.

Upfront costs

  • Design £1,000 to £2,500
  • Domain £2.99 to £100 / year
  • SSL certificate £15 to £250 / year
  • Total:  £1,017.99 to £2,850

Ongoing costs

  • Hosting £10 to £350 / month
  • Content Updates £0 to £60 / month
  • Total:  £10 to £410 / month

Bespoke website

Bespoke websites are great for larger companies requiring more functionality, with a higher number of visitors or with more information to share.

I know what you’re thinking – surely all websites are bespoke? Well, that’s not necessarily true.

For smaller websites without any complex requirements, a web designer can normally repurpose an existing web template for your business. In this case, the website is tailored, but not bespoke.

A truly bespoke website is built from scratch to an original and unique set of requirements.

Need a bespoke website with ecommerce functionality? Take a look at the section above for a breakdown of ecommerce website costs.

Bespoke websites are database-driven. They have advanced functionality and unlimited subpages (pages created within the subcategories of your site).

They will almost always be required by businesses in certain industries, including financial services, recruitment or property companies, health and fitness businesses, and architects. This list is by no means exhaustive – any business requiring complex functionality may well find that they fall into the bespoke price bracket.

Bespoke websites will also take mobile responsiveness to the next level, creating a ‘fluid’ design that may look completely different on mobile.

As over 50% of searches are conducted on mobile, making this experience as great as possible for your customers should definitely be a priority.

When requesting a bespoke website, it is highly beneficial to work with a dedicated partner. Unless you have a strong relationship with a talented freelancer, we would definitely recommend looking for an agency to work with. This ensures you are getting the best possible service and that they understand your business and what you are trying to achieve. It’s even more vital that you get a quote for the project as a whole, rather than a ‘per hour’ rate. This is because larger projects have the potential to produce time consuming problems that are out of your control as a customer.
