About Webflow

Webflow is a web design tool, CMS (Content Management System), and hosting platform all in one. All without requiring users to write code HTML, CSS or JavaScript code.

The company offers a free Webflow University with lots of learning materials.

The Webflow Dashboard

This is the central hub where you can manage all your Webflow projects. It provides quick access to your sites, account settings, billing information, and team management tools.

The Dashoard provides:

  • Sites tab: Here, you can create new projects or access existing ones. Each project thumbnail offers a quick view of the site and options for settings, sharing, and publishing.
  • Templates: Webflow offers a variety of templates. You can browse, preview, and select templates directly from the dashboard to kickstart your design process.

The Webflow Workspace

This offers the following elements:

  • Elements Panel: This is where all the building blocks for your website are located. Elements are categorized under Layout, Typography, Media, Forms, and Components for easy access.
  • Navigator: This shows a hierarchical tree structure of your website, making it easy to select, reposition, or nest elements within your page. You can drag elements to rearrange their order.
  • Style Manager: Theis is where you control all the styling aspects of the selected element. It mirrors the properties you would typically set using CSS. You can create, apply, and manage reusable classes here. Styles applied to a class in one instance automatically update all elements using that class across your site. You can manage global fonts and colours from the Style Manager to ensure consistency throughout your site.
  • Settings: Each element in your design has specific settings that can be accessed through the right-click menu or a settings icon in the editor. These settings can include link settings, form settings, and various attributes like alt text for images. At page level, you can manage SEO settings, custom code, background videos, and more.
  • Interactions Panel: Located typically on the right side of the workspace, this panel allows you to add and manage interactions and animations. It is designed to let you visually create complex animations without code.
  • Assets Manager: This is where all your images, videos, and other files are stored. You can upload, manage, and apply assets directly from this panel.

Previewing and publishing your website

  • Preview Mode: Allows you to view and interact with your site as it would appear to a visitor, which is essential for testing interactions and layout responsiveness.
  • Publish: Located at the top right of the interface, this button lets you publish your site so it can be viewed live on the web.

Getting started with Webflow

Follow these steps:

  1. Go to Webflow and click the Get started - it's free button.
  2. Enter your details as requested
  3. You will be asked Who do you want to build websites for?. Choose Myself.
  4. Next, you are asked Are you a student?. Choose No.
  5. Webflow now asks What type of site are you looking to build today? Answer with Business site.
  6. When asked Are you interested in hiring someone..., click No.

Webflow begins the process of building your website. This will take a few moments.

Working with the Webflow Designer

Follow these steps:

  1. You may be prompted if you want to Build a simple layout. Click Exit to close the dialog box.
  2. You are asked Select a way to get started. Choose Blank site.
  3. Enter your Site name and click Create site.

As a few moments, you are shown the Designer screen.

Exploring the Webflow Templates

Follow these steps:

  1. At the top left of the Webflow Designer screen, click the Webflow icon to display the Webflow Workspace.
  2. In the menu at the top of the screen, click Marketplace and then Templates. Explote the free and commercial templates available.
  3. When finished, click Dashboard in the menu at the top of the screen.
  4. Click your website to open it again in the Webflow Designer
  5. Click Add layout to begin building your website.
