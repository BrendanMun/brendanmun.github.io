Colour of the Year 2020: Classic Blue

The votes have been counted, the results are in, and we have a winner: the choice of Colour of the Year 2020 goes to Classic Blue, a shade somewhere between royal blue and navy. Or, as expressed in the six-character hexadecimal code used by web designers and front-end developers, #004680.

Every twelve months, Pantone, creators of the world’s most widely-used colour identification system, delivers its verdict on what colour will best catch the mood of the year ahead.

It is a measure of the company’s influence on visual culture that its latest annual choice attracted attention in places as diverse as CNN and Time, New York’s Metropolitan Museum of Art, the UK’s Homes & Property website, and the Net magazine’s Creative Blog. All evidence of Pantone’s pervasive influence across sectors that include fashion, home furnishings, product packaging and, of course, web and mobile front-end design.

Software developers Adobe greeted the choice as “a welcome refuge for our uncertain era ... Blue hues, long associated with peace, calm, and the dependable daylight sky, feel solid and reliable.” The creators of Photoshop even put together a short video to celebrate the “constancy and comfort that Classic Blue offers us.”

Not everyone is a fan, however. “Awful, forgettable, pedestrian”, complained US technology and business magazine Fast Company. Also unimpressed was the London-based Dezeen, the world’s most popular and influential journal of architecture: “Pantone played it safe and still missed the mark” was their verdict on what it condemned as a “depressingly cynical” choice.

The world’s favourite colour

Research in recent decades has confirmed what Dublin GAA supporters have always believed: blue is the world’s favourite colour. In marketing and advertising, it has long been the go-to choice for organisations who want to promote trust in their products and services – and generally convey a sense of dependability, sincerity, and calm competence.

Take a look at the websites and mobile apps of many of the world’s top IT brands. In terms of colour, the sight is not too different from Croke Park’s Hill 16 on All-Ireland Sunday.

A reassuring backdrop in changing times

As you can see from the image below, Pantone’s selection of Classic Blue for 2020 marks a departure from its choices of recent years.

But it also represents a return to the company’s very first Colour of the Year, announced in the final month of the last millennium. Then the prize went to Cerulean, a shade of blue that captured both the excitement and anxieties presented by a new decade.

Explains Laurie Pressman, VP of the Pantone Color Institute: “From now and then [1999], there’s the same feeling of trepidation in our global culture. From an emotional, psychological standpoint, blue has always represented a certain amount of calm and dependability.”

She continues: “Blue is a colour that you can rely on.” And in “a time that requires trust and faith and confidence”, it offers “an anchoring foundation on which to build as we cross the threshold into a new era.”
